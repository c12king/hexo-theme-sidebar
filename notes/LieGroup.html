1. Denitions of the Lie group and the Lie algebra
Before starting with the Lie groups, it is necessary to introduce some basic
knowledge of the manifold theory. The term manifold could mean a large class
of mathematical objects, among which the simplest one is the topological manifold.
More frequently, it means a topological manifold together with some additional
structure.
Denition 1.1 Topological Manifold is a Hausdor topological space which is
also second countable and locally Euclidean.
The one we use here is the dierential structure and smooth structure.
Denition 1.2 Smooth Manifold is a topological manifold M together with a
smooth structure on it.
Denition 1.3 Lie Group A real (complex) Lie group is a group which is also
a nite-dimensional real (complex) smooth manifold, and in which the group oper-
ations of multiplication and inversion are smooth maps.
Two basic varieties of Lie groups: the simple and the solvable.
Denition 1.4 Simple Lie groups have the property that they regenerate them-
selves under commutation. In other words, they are connected non-abelian Lie
groups which do not have nontrivial connected normal subgroups.
Denition 1.5 Solvable Lie groups are not simple (Lie group), and each one
contain a chain of subgroups, each of which is an invariant subgroup of its prede-
cessor.
Simple and solvable Lie groups are the building blocks for all other Lie groups.
Semisimple Lie groups are direct products of simple Lie groups. Non-semisimple Lie
groups are semi-direct products of (semi)simple Lie groups with invariant subgroups
that are solvable.
Not surprisingly, solvable Lie groups are related to the integrability, or at least
simplication, of ordinary dierential equations. However, simple Lie groups are
more rigidly constrained, and form such a beautiful subject of study in their own
right that much of the eort of the mathematicians during the last century involved
the classication and complete enumeration of all simple Lie groups and the discus-
sion of their properties. Even today, there is no complete classication of solvable
Lie groups, and therefore non-semisimple Lie groups.
Denition 1.6 Compactness A topological space is compact if every open cover
has a nite sub-cover.
Denition 1.7 Connected A topological space is said to be disconnected if it is
the union of two disjoint nonempty open sets. Otherwise, it is said to be connected.
